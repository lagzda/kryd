General Examination

We will carry out a thorough examination to check the overall health of your mouth, including your teeth, gums and surrounding tissues. Where necessary, we will take X-rays and intra-oral photos to aid our diagnosis.

X-Rays

X-Rays are used to look for dental problems not visible to the naked eye, such as cavities between teeth or under existing fillings.

Go Back

OPG

Our OPG X-ray machine enables us to take a scan of the entire upper and lower jaw at the same time scanning the teeth and jaw bone from ear to ear. It shows facial bones, teeth and sinuses all in one image.

Periodontal Examination

When on the general examination the dentist finds that you are susceptible to gum disease, the Comprehensive Periodontal Evaluation, or CPE, is the best way to assess your periodontal health in depth.

Dental Hygiene

Dental Hygiene is a very important treatment helping patients to maintain healthy teeth and gums. We offer a range of hygiene treatments that are designed to keep your teeth and gums strong and healthy, prevent oral diseases and preserve your beautiful smile.

Go Back

Fissure Sealant

The crinkles and dips in the tooth structure are called pits and fissures. Pits and fissures create hiding places for plaque bacteria, called stagnation areas, these bacteria feed on a sugary diet which in turn leads to tooth decay. This is purely a preventative measure, which enables a smooth surface for the patient to clean effectively and a reduction in the need for pit and fissure restorations (fillings). It is usually placed in the pits and fissures of back teeth, where brushing is difficult.

Go Back

Fluoride

Exposure to fluoride makes the tooth structure stronger. Some patients will benefit from additional fluoride treatment designed to strengthen tooth enamel and make it less likely to decay. Use of a fluoride treatment is considered most crucial for children and adolescents, whose teeth are still developing.

Go Back

Periodontal Treatment

Your gums are like cushions for your teeth: they surround the teeth in the jaws and help to support them. Looking after your gums is just as important as looking after your teeth. Red and swollen gums that bleed when you brush them could be a sign of gum disease, also known as gingivitis. Gingivitis is often caused by poor oral hygiene. If caught in the early stages, it can be reversed with professional dental treatment (hygiene appointment) followed by good oral health care.

Root Canal Treatment

Root canal treatment (also called Endodontics) is needed when the pulp inside your tooth becomes infected through tooth decay or damaged by an injury to your mouth. This infection may spread through the root canal system, which could eventually lead to an abscess, causing a great deal of discomfort. If root canal treatment is not carried out, the tooth may need to be taken out. Our dentists can perform root canal treatment to stop the infection from spreading and will preserve as much of your tooth as possible.

Go Back

Crowns

A crown is a type of dental restoration used to fix teeth that have been broken, weakened by decay or have a very large filling. The crown fits over the remaining part of a prepared tooth, making it strong and giving it the shape of a natural tooth. It will also be tooth coloured, giving the restoration a more natural appearance so it fits in with the rest of your teeth.

Go Back

Inlays/Onlays

Inlays and onlays are two types of restorations that your dentist can suggest if you have a cavity in your tooth that needs to be filled. An inlay is a small filling that fits into the biting surface of a tooth while an onlay covers a larger area of the tooth and is better suited to bigger cavities.

Go Back

Bridge

If you require a more extensive restoration than a crown or have more than one tooth that needs to be repaired, a good solution is a bridge. Bridges are usually made of a precious metal base which gives them a solid structure and makes them hard-wearing. If you need a bridge in your smile line, porcelain is usually added to the base to make it look more like real teeth.

Go Back

Denture

If you have a few or all of your teeth missing, one solution is to have a denture. A denture is made from hard-wearing material that mimics the look of teeth and gums to give you mouth a natural appearance. Your denture will be made to fit snugly on your gums and you will be able to remove it for cleaning.

White Fillings

One option to improve the cosmetic look of your smile is to have your metal- coloured fillings replaced with tooth-coloured or "white" fillings. This is a safe procedure that entails one of our dentists removing any decay from your tooth, taking out the metal or amalgam filling and placing a tooth coloured filling instead.

Go Back

Veneers

Are your teeth stained, chipped, or not aligned on top of each other? Veneers are a good treatment option to consider because they look like natural teeth and don't require a major procedure.

Whitening

Do you have stained teeth that make you feel self-conscious about smiling? Teeth whitening can be a good solution for certain types of tooth staining.

Go Back

Botox

Frequent facial contractions combined with other environmental and lifestyle factors are responsible for lines and wrinkles that eventually become permanent. Botulinum toxin restricts facial muscle movement which means signs of age can be prevented.

Go Back

Dermal Fillers

Over time your skin loses the collagen that helps to shape and support it. By replacing the lost collagen, dermal fillers reduce lines and wrinkles to give a more youthful looking appearance. The effects are immediate, non invasive and last for several months.

Single Implant

A single tooth implant is used when there is a single tooth missing requiring replacement. Ideally the implant should be placed shortly after the tooth has been lost to minimise the amount of bone loss in the area.

Go Back

Short Implant Bridge

A short implant supported bridge is very similar to that of a conventional bridge. The only difference being that the support of the bridge is provided by the implants as opposed to the natural roots.

Go Back

Retained Overdenture

Due to the negative points associated with dentures patients are always looking for an option to stabilise the denture allowing it to feel a lot more comfortable. The overdenture is the solution for this problem. The bespoke appliance is made of an acrylic material with custom teeth and a metal reinforcement supported by two or four implants.

Go Back

Invisalign

If your teeth are crowded, unevenly spaced, or have shifted since you last wore corrective braces, Invisalign "invisible" braces can help you attain that arresting smile that only straightened teeth can give you. Results can usually be seen between 12 to 18 months.

Go Back

Six Month Smiles

6 Month Smiles treatments use state-of- the-art technology and methods to help move teeth quickly and comfortably. The treatment involves using nickel titanium wires, which are coloured like natural teeth, to help move the teeth that are usually on show when somebody smiles. This means that treatment is effective and efficient. Results can usually be seen between 6 and 9 months.

Go Back

Inman Aligner

A fantastic alternative to other orthodontic appliances, the Inman Aligner is a simple way of being able to straighten your front teeth and is great choice for adults who experience a relapse of tooth movement. The Inman Aligner is a specially made, custom appliance, which gently moves teeth into the desired position without discomfort. Results can usually be seen between 6 and 18 weeks.
